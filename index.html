Rayla

I can definitively say that I know the difference between a kiss that means something and a kiss that doesn’t. In my experience, as awesome as kissing is, it is so much more awesome when it comes with genuinely caring about the other person, and knowing that they genuinely care about you. I’ve found that when I’m more casual about kissing, I forget about the most important things that I look for in a relationship, qualities that are more lasting and important than physical attraction. These qualities, such as trust, consistency, and mutual respect, are what love is founded on. And if there’s one thing that I know, it’s that there is no replacement for love. Love is what fills in all the voids in my life, and it’s become so important to me that I can't afford to be casual about it.

Ryan

I've been in plenty of relationships, many of them good, but a few of them bad. For the ones that went sour I could always trace the source back to me becoming less interested in who they were, and more interested in what their bodies could offer me. When I think back on these things I could tell that I really wasn't interested in those girls from the beginning, and simply allowed my base physical desire to be the driving force behind dating them for as long as I did. I realized that I needed to treat woman more as trusted companions and not as objects of physical pleasure. To go around having NCMOs would only cloud my judgment as to what I really want, and get myself lost in my own base emotions again. That is something that has lead former girlfriends to feel taken advantage of, and me feeling terrible because of how I treated them.

Caleb

I think most people want to love and be loved. Dating has a singular purpose of helping two people find in each other the qualities of character and affection they are looking for in a love-based marriage/relationship. Physical and sexual affection (not just sex, but kissing, holding hands, and making out) is a big part of developing that healthy loving relationship. However, when it is applied too early or too often in a developing relationship, it will rob both people of the opportunities they need to ascertain the necessary qualities to succeed in marriage. Some people may find a successful relationship from a NCMO, but I and most people I think, have lost a lot of potentially wonderful relationships from a fear or unwillingness to hold off on physical and sexual aspects of the dating process and invest more into discovering who the person really is.

Frequently Asked Questions

We try to answer some of the most common questions we get while sharing our brand

What is a NCMO?
A NCMO is an acronym that stands for Non-Committal Make Out. It’s something that some young people view as a way to relieve stress or tension, without explicitly having sex. However, NCMOs, although a better alternative to sex, is yet another way people can objectify others, which distracts them from what is truly important. When we meet someone, we should be looking to make a connection, not a way to simply satisfy our physical needs.

What are some negative side effects of a NCMO?
NCMOs can lead people to objectify others. Instead of focusing on who a person is, and what kind of personality or talents they have, those who subconsciously objectify others may put what the person looks like above all of that. A healthy relationship does involve physical intimacy, but if someone is focusing too much on someone else's body, and not on who they are as a person, relationships can lead to heartbreak and insecurity. Also, many times when two people are having habitual NCMOs with each other, one may develop feelings for the other, while the other is simply “having fun”. Something like this can also cause those same feelings of heartbreak and insecurity as sharing your body in such a way also holds feelings of trust and romance, whether we want them there or not. We understand not everyone may feel these effects, and we understand that everything affects someone differently; however, in our varying experiences ranging from our friends and families to our own personal selves, we have seen these facts to be self-evident.

Don’t you think you’re making a bigger deal out of this than it really is?
That is exactly the danger of NCMOs. A lot of people think NCMOs won't effect them personally, or that they are innoncent acts that don't mean much. However, NCMOs can train the brain to treat people a certain way and can even be so subtle that you won't notice it until it is a much bigger problem than it could have been.

Are you saying I am a bad person, just because I participate in NCMOs?
Please do not believe we think less of anyone who has or currently does participate in NCMOs. Our movement isn’t about judging people or telling them what kind of person they are. Our movement always has and always will be about raising awareness. Our purpose for starting this movement is simply to help people understand what something like a NCMO can do to their mental and emotional health. Many times, people don’t realize the effects that NCMOs are having on them until it is too late. We wish only to help people avoid unnecessary heart ache and focus on the things that will make them happy in the long term. If you feel offended or attacked, we sincerely apologize, for that is not our intension.
